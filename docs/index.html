representation: breaks ✗

The upshot. The Bloch sphere is a one-qubit miracle — at 1 qubit it's the whole truth, at 2 it hangs on only with a coupling sphere bolted alongside, and at 3 it goes blind: it simply can't show all the interactions at once.

So how do you level up? The first step is the basics of (not-so-)complex numbers. They're actually pretty simple — and they greatly simplify not just quantum mechanics but big swaths of engineering, chemistry, physics, and math. Saying “I want to understand quantum, but I don't want to understand complex numbers” is about like saying “I want to do woodworking, but I don't want to learn the saw.” Once the numbers click, a quantum state is just a table of these arrows — its amplitudes.
