What am I looking at? (the big idea)

A Bloch sphere shows one qubit. The arrow (Bloch vector r⃗) is the qubit's reduced state.

Separable states → both arrows have length 1 (each qubit has a definite state). Entangled states → the arrows shrink; a maximally entangled Bell state puts both arrows at the center (maximally mixed, r⃗ = 0).

The centre coupling sphere colours each measurement direction n̂ by the joint correlation E(n̂) = ⟨(n̂·σ)(n̂·σ)⟩: green = correlated, red = anti-correlated, dim = uncorrelated. The surface also bulges out where correlated and pinches toward the centre where not, so strength reads as shape. The panel's ⟨XX⟩/⟨YY⟩/⟨ZZ⟩ are just E along the three coloured axis pokes.

So the singlet Ψ⁻ is a uniformly red round ball (anti-correlated every direction — its rotational invariance), Φ⁺ is a vivid green ball with red caps, and a product state like |00⟩ is a green dumbbell along the z-axis only. A round, fully-bulged ball while the arrows vanish = maximal entanglement. (Concurrence C stays in the panel; |r⃗| = √(1 − C²).)
